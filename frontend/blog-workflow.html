Part 1

Manual publishing journey

Human research, writing, validation, and CMS publishing with repeated handoffs.

Before AI

Manual workflow overview

Current state

The current process depends on an admin or content editor to constantly monitor trusted health sources, identify important updates, write articles, review medical accuracy, and publish each post manually inside the CMS.

That approach keeps content curated, but it is slow, repetitive, and difficult to scale when the number of health sources and important updates grows.

Before AI

Roles involved

Admin / Content Manager

Monitors global health sources and identifies relevant developments.

Content Writer

Turns research findings into a structured blog article.

Medical Editor

Checks terminology, facts, and clarity before publication.

Publisher

Creates or schedules the blog entry in the platform CMS.

In many cases one administrator performs multiple roles, which increases operational pressure and reduces throughput.

Before AI

Step-by-step manual process

01

Monitor sources

Check WHO, CDC, NIH, journals, research portals, and health news platforms.

02

Identify relevant updates

Shortlist medically relevant topics such as outbreaks, vaccines, approvals, and policy alerts.

03

Collect information

Extract facts, compare sources, save references, and organize notes manually.

04

Write article

Create the title, introduction, key developments, healthcare impact, and conclusion.

05

Review and edit

Validate medical terminology, statistics, readability, and source accuracy.

06

Create in CMS

Enter title, content, category, tags, author data, and any supporting media.

07

Publish

Publish immediately or schedule the article for later release.

Before AI

Key pain points

  • Time-consuming: each article can take from 90 minutes to 3 hours.
  • Repetitive: the same monitoring, copying, summarizing, and publishing tasks repeat daily.
  • Risk of missed updates: important developments can be overlooked if a source is missed.
  • Limited scalability: publishing multiple timely articles per day becomes difficult.
  • Delayed publishing: critical health information may appear too late for readers.
  • Human dependency: the workflow relies heavily on a small number of people.

Part 2

AI automation journey

Automated monitoring, filtering, generation, and publishing with faster turnaround.

Using AI

AI automated workflow overview

Target state

The Global Health Intelligence Agent automates monitoring, topic filtering, content analysis, article generation, and publishing so the platform can share timely health updates with minimal administrative effort.

Instead of depending on manual research and writing, the system continuously gathers updates from trusted sources and converts them into structured content for healthcare professionals.

  • Example trusted sources: World Health Organization updates, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention alerts, National Institutes of Health research updates, The Lancet, Nature Medicine, and global health news APIs.

Using AI

Objectives of the AI agent

Automate monitoring

Track trusted global health news and research continuously.

Filter importance

Surface updates relevant to healthcare professionals.

Generate articles

Create structured blog drafts automatically.

Publish faster

Reduce time from discovery to publication dramatically.

Reduce workload

Remove repetitive research and drafting tasks from admins.

Scale output

Support multiple articles per day without proportional manual effort.

Using AI

Step-by-step AI process

01

Scheduled execution

Run on cron intervals such as every 30 minutes, hourly, or daily.

02

Collect updates

Fetch news from APIs, RSS feeds, and trusted medical sources.

03

Filter topics

Keep infectious diseases, vaccine news, drug approvals, research, and public health alerts.

04

Detect duplicates

Check title similarity, topic overlap, time window, and source duplication.

05

Analyze with AI

Extract findings, statistics, clinical relevance, and core impact.

06

Generate the blog

Produce title, introduction, developments, healthcare impact, and conclusion.

07

Store article

Save the generated record with draft, pending review, or published status.

08

Publish or review

Auto-publish immediately or route to an editor for approval.

Using AI

Key benefits

  • Continuous monitoring: the platform watches global updates 24/7.
  • Faster publishing: important information can move to publication almost immediately.
  • Reduced manual work: administrators spend less time searching and writing.
  • Scalable generation: more articles can be produced without increasing operational load.
  • Consistent structure: AI output follows a stable, professional article format.
